Senior Deputy Governor

Dr. Neelam Dhungana Timsina

Senior Deputy Governor, Nepal Rastra Bank

As the Senior Deputy Governor of Nepal Rastra Bank, a position I have held since March 9, 2021, I firmly believe that honesty, perseverance, and hard work are the cornerstones of shaping one's identity. In my view, moral integrity and honesty are the true treasures in a person's life. During my tenure, I have played a pivotal role in helping Nepal Rastra Bank achieve its objectives. My contributions have spanned across various domains, including the development of a secure payment system, the successful regulation and supervision of banks and financial institutions, the creation and execution of monetary policy, and the management of foreign exchange.

To enhance the formulation of monetary policy, Nepal Rastra Bank established a Monetary Policy Advisory Committee, which includes participation from external experts.

Recently, to enhance the formulation of monetary policy, Nepal Rastra Bank established a Monetary Policy Advisory Committee, which includes participation from external experts. I have had the privilege of leading this committee as its coordinator. The committee provides recommendations on the stance, objectives, instruments, and monetary aggregates for future monetary policies, considering both domestic and global economic conditions. Under my coordinated leadership, significant initiatives have been taken to improve foreign exchange management. These efforts include facilitating debt and investment processes, streamlining principal and interest repayments, attracting foreign investment, and encouraging remittance inflows. Through efficient portfolio management of foreign exchange reserves, Nepal has seen substantial benefits. Additionally, I have served as the coordinator of the Microfinance Victims' Problem Resolution Committee, established by the Government of Nepal to address issues related to microfinance. The committee's report has paved the way for resolving these challenges

My father was a secondary school inspector, and my mother was a school teacher. In our household, we managed all the domestic tasks ourselves, from caring for livestock to growing vegetables and helping in the kitchen.

In my career, I have also served as the Chairperson of the Credit Information Center, a member of the Executive Committee of the South East Asian Central Banks Research and Training Centre (SEACEN) in Malaysia, a member of the SAARC Finance Payment Council, an alternate director on the board of the Alliance for Financial Inclusion (AFI), and an editor for the Economic Review published by Nepal Rastra Bank. The accomplishments achieved through the honest and diligent fulfillment of my duties have undeniably propelled the economy forward, encouraged the productive sector, uplifted impoverished and marginalized communities, supported the Government of Nepal's economic development initiatives, and ultimately contributed to Nepal Rastra Bank's success. Witnessing these positive impacts fills me with profound pride

I was born on November 29, 1968 (14th Mangsir, 2025 BS) in Damak Municipality, Jhapa, Ward No. 13. From a young age, I was a diligent student. My father was a secondary school inspector, and my mother was a school teacher. In our household, we managed all the domestic tasks ourselves, from caring for livestock to growing vegetables and helping in the kitchen. Time management was challenging, especially with the one-hour walk to school. Despite these challenges, the educational atmosphere at home instilled in me a strong sense of duty to use my education for the benefit of my country.

I completed my School Leaving Certificate (SLC) from Himalaya Secondary School in Damak in 2041 BS. I then graduated from Damak Campus, topping my district in the 2042-2044 batch with an I. Com. Following this, I earned my B. Com from Mahendra Morang Campus in Biratnagar during the 2045-2047 period. In 2050 BS, I completed my MBA with a gold medal, graduating with first-class honors.

I began my professional journey as a mentor in Accounting at the Postgraduate Campus in Biratnagar from Bhadra 2053 to Mangsir 2054 BS. During this time, an opening for the position of Assistant Officer at Nepal Rastra Bank arose. I applied, passed the examination, and commenced my service at Nepal Rastra Bank on 22nd Mangsir 2054 BS. As an Officer III, I contributed to the Foreign Exchange Management Department, Biratnagar Office, and the Bank Supervision Departmen

I emphasized effective supervision to strengthen financial stability, including institutional governance, internal controls, and risk management within banks.

While diligently performing my duties, I had the opportunity to pursue an MA in Economics at the University of Wollongong in Australia, graduating with distinction. In Baisakh 2061 BS, I was promoted to Officer Second through an internal competitive examination, making significant contributions to the Monetary and Government Finance Branch of the Economic Research Department

During my tenure, I honed my skills in research methodologies, quantitative modeling techniques, writing, and preparing monetary surveys and economic reports, all of which have been instrumental in guiding the institution toward its objectives. In 2067 BS, I was promoted to Officer I through another internal competitive examination. While serving as an Officer I in the Bank Supervision Department, I emphasized effective supervision to strengthen financial stability, including institutional governance, internal controls, and risk management within banks.

In 2074 BS, I was promoted to Officer Special, serving in the Internal Audit Department and the Public Debt (now Monetary Management) Department. My tenure in the Internal Audit Department allowed me to closely observe the central bank's internal control systems and understand the necessary governance inspections within the banking and financial sector. Despite the general reluctance among employees to transfer to the Internal Audit Department, I eagerly took on the role of department head. I led the creation of process documents, an Audit Charter, Risk-Based Internal Audit protocols, and the Three Lines of Defense Model, establishing a robust internal audit framework. I also facilitated foreign training opportunities for staff, enhancing their understanding of internal auditing's importance within the central bank. As a result, employee motivation increased, and they now take pride in being transferred to the Internal Audit Department.

Subsequently, I was transferred to the Monetary Management Department, where I contributed significantly to liquidity management, the effective implementation of the interest rate corridor, and the practical application of monetary policy through open market operations, crucial in achieving price and interest rate stability. Amidst these responsibilities, I completed my Ph.D. in 2019 on "A Study on Bank Lending Behavior and Monetary Transmission Mechanism in Nepalese Commercial Banks" from Mewar University in India.

Recognizing my inner talents, capabilities, extensive knowledge in economic, monetary, and banking affairs, unwavering dedication, and leadership abilities, the Government of Nepal appointed me as the Senior Deputy Governor of Nepal Rastra Bank on Falgun 25, 2077. I have diligently and skillfully fulfilled this responsibility ever since.
